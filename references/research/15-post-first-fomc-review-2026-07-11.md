# Post-first-FOMC review: observed Warsh chair behavior

Research cutoff: 2026-07-11.

Purpose: replace speculation about Warsh's first FOMC with evidence-based calibration. Keep `14-recent-developments-2026-06-17.md` as the frozen pre-release prediction; use this file for observed behavior after the statement, SEP, press conference, and minutes became available.

## Official record

### Decision and communication

- The FOMC voted 12-0 to maintain the federal funds target range at 3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent.
- The statement was materially shorter, removed language suggesting an easing bias, and gave no forward guidance.
- It described solid activity, strong productivity and investment, stable unemployment, and inflation still above 2 percent partly because of supply shocks.
- It ended with a direct institutional commitment to deliver price stability.

Sources:

- https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm
- https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260617.htm

### SEP and forecast posture

- Warsh encouraged other participants to submit projections but did not submit his own.
- He described dots as forecasts written with pencils and large erasers: conditional judgments, not promises.
- Median 2026 projections were 2.2 percent real GDP growth, 4.3 percent unemployment, 3.6 percent PCE inflation, 3.3 percent core PCE inflation, and a 3.8 percent year-end federal funds rate.
- The published median therefore implied a mildly firmer year-end path, not easing.

Sources:

- https://www.federalreserve.gov/mediacenter/files/FOMCpresconf20260617.pdf
- https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/files/fomcprojtabl20260617.pdf

### Five task forces

Warsh announced independent task forces covering:

1. Fed communications.
2. The Fed balance sheet.
3. Use of and reliance on existing data sources.
4. Productivity and jobs in an era of transformation.
5. The Fed's inflation frameworks.

The method matters: start from first principles, use internal and external expertise, examine alternatives, and submit proposals for policymaker consideration. This is reform through bounded institutional process, not unilateral chair action.

Source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/mediacenter/files/FOMCpresconf20260617.pdf

### Balance-sheet composition

- The Committee reaffirmed an ample-reserves regime.
- It directed the Desk to reinvest principal payments from agency securities into Treasury bills.
- This changes SOMA composition and duration without using the policy rate to signal macro accommodation.

Sources:

- https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a1.htm
- https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260617.htm

### Inflation diagnosis

- Minutes attributed higher inflation to tariffs, Middle East supply disruption, energy/input costs, and AI-related demand.
- Several participants saw broader price pressure beyond energy and tariffs, while longer-term expectations generally remained anchored.
- Inflation risks were tilted upward, but the Committee did not respond with an immediate hike.
- BEA later reported May PCE inflation at 4.1 percent and core PCE inflation at 3.4 percent year over year.

Sources:

- https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260617.htm
- https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/personal-income-and-outlays-may-2026

### Independence and coordination

Warsh described monetary policy as independent while supporting regular Fed chair-Treasury secretary meetings. Information exchange does not itself imply fiscal dominance when statutory roles and decision authority remain delineated. His operating formulation is a wide informational lens with a narrower policy remit.

Source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/mediacenter/files/FOMCpresconf20260617.pdf

## Backtest against the pre-release skill

### Strong matches

- **Chair sequencing:** The skill expected reform through sequencing rather than a one-meeting rate rupture. Warsh held rates and launched structured reviews.
- **Consensus discipline:** The skill warned against over-predicting dissent. The decision and statement passed 12-0.
- **Price-stability capital:** Warsh made delivery of price stability the central institutional promise.
- **Communication as operations:** Statement compression, removal of the easing bias, and elimination of forward guidance changed the reaction-function signal.
- **Propagation test:** The official discussion separated supply shocks, broader persistence, and anchored expectations.

### Underestimated dimensions

- Communication reform was faster than expected: the statement was substantially shortened immediately.
- SEP skepticism became observable behavior: Warsh withheld his own dot while preserving colleagues' participation.
- Balance-sheet composition moved immediately through agency-to-Treasury-bill reinvestment.
- Task-force governance emerged as a signature chair operating method.

Score: strong match on stance and institutional reasoning, with meaningful underprediction of reform speed and balance-sheet composition action.

## Skill calibration

1. **Commitment is not an immediate rate move.** A chair can harden the inflation regime, remove easing guidance, and hold rates while evidence develops.
2. **Consensus is an output.** In chair mode, a 12-0 statement can be more informative than a dramatic dissent.
3. **Forecast humility is not data nihilism.** Distinguish the Committee's SEP, participant projections, and the chair's decision to abstain.
4. **Composition is policy.** Inspect security type, maturity, reinvestment destination, and reserve regime, not just total assets.
5. **Reform through task forces.** Expect bounded review, external expertise, staff work, and later policymaker choice.
6. **Wide lens, narrow remit.** Information exchange with Treasury can coexist with monetary-policy independence.
